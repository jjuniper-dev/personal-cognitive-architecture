You have high anxiety triggered by meetings and bilaterals. **This anxiety prevents you from focusing on anything else until the meeting is over.**

Understanding why this matters:

```
Without meeting anxiety management:
- Meeting at 10am → anxious until noon → can't focus 10am-3pm

```

Your problem isn't willpower or "anxiety management therapy." Your problem is **workload design that doesn't account for your anxiety**.

## Part 1: Understanding Your Anxiety Constraint

### What's Happening

**Pre-meeting anxiety**: As a meeting approaches, anxiety builds
- Your mind is already in the meeting before it starts
- You can't focus on other work because you're rehearsing, worrying, preparing
- Attention is fragmented between current task and upcoming meeting

**During meeting anxiety**: You're present, but anxious
- High social/cognitive load
- Hyperawareness of yourself and others
- Decision-making under stress

**Post-meeting anxiety**: You can't shift focus
- Your mind is still replaying the meeting
- You're overthinking what was said and what you said
- Adrenaline is still elevated; can't settle into other work
- Recovery required: 2-4 hours minimum

### Why This Matters for Your OS

Your OS is **sequential + internal-processing + burst-energy**.

Anxiety adds another layer:
- **Attention**: Anxiety fractures whatever attention model you have (sequential becomes scattered)
- **Processing**: Internal processing mode breaks down; can't think clearly
- **Energy**: Anxiety drains energy faster than normal work (1 hour meeting = 2-3 hours of energy cost)

**Combined effect**: One meeting removes half your day from the productive schedule.

### Your Sustainable Load

Based on your anxiety profile:

**Maximum: 1 meeting per day, 2-3 per week**

```

Tuesday: 1 meeting (11am) + recovery → day lost  
Wednesday: NO meeting → protected deep work day
Thursday: 1 meeting (11am) + recovery → day lost
```

With 1 meeting per day:
- **Monday**: Meeting + recovery (day lost)

- **Friday**: Light work (recovery from week)

This gives you ONE protected deep work day (Wed) + manageable load.

With 4-5 per week:
- You lose 4-5 days to meeting anxiety
- Only 0-1 days fully protected
- Recovery extends into evenings and next week
- Unsustainable

## Part 4: Structure for Meeting Days vs. No-Meeting Days

### No-Meeting Days (2-3 per week)

Use your standard schedule:

```
6am-10am:   DEEP WORK (your golden window)
10am-12pm:  Routine + admin
12pm-1pm:   Lunch + break
1pm-3pm:    Routine + light work (or continue deep work if momentum)
3pm-5pm:    Light work or stop
5pm+:       OFF
```

**Goal**: Maximize deep work output on these days.

### Meeting Days (1-2 per week)

Completely different schedule:

```
6am-11am:   DEEP WORK (before anxiety hits)
            - Get something meaningful done
            - Stop at 10:45am to prep/ground for meeting
            - 15 min: breathing, reviewing agenda, grounding technique
            
11am-12pm:  MEETING
            - 1 bilateral or team meeting only
            - Max 60 minutes
            - (If it's back-to-back, that's a mistake; don't schedule this way)
            
12pm-4pm:   MANDATORY RECOVERY BLOCK (2-4 hours)
            - Walk outside (30-60 min minimum)
            - Lunch away from desk
            - Light tasks: email sorting, organizing (no decisions, no thinking)
            - Absolutely no deep work
            - Absolutely no new decisions
            - Goal: return to baseline anxiety, not accomplish work
            - If still anxious at 3pm: extend recovery; don't force work
            
4pm-5pm:    OPTIONAL light work (only if fully recovered)
            - If recovered: light email or routine tasks
            - If still anxious: continue recovery
            
5pm+:       OFF (gentle recovery continues)
            - Outdoor activity (proven to help anxiety recovery)
            - Personal time
            - No work
```

**Key difference**: On meeting days, **recovery is your only job from 12-4pm**. Not "try to work while recovering." Just recover.

## Part 5: Meeting Prep & Decompression Rituals

These aren't optional. They reduce pre/post meeting anxiety.

### Pre-Meeting Ritual (15 min, before meeting starts)

**Timing**: Stop working at 10:45am for a 11am meeting

**Steps**:
1. **Step away** — Leave your desk, go to kitchen/bathroom
2. **Breathe** (2 min) — 4-count in, 6-count out, 5 times (lowers cortisol)
3. **Review agenda** (3 min) — What's the meeting about? Any curveballs?
4. **Settle yourself** (5 min) — Sit quietly; ground yourself in your body
5. **One positive thought** (1 min) — "I can handle this" or "This will be okay"
6. **Return to meeting** — You're grounded, not anxious

**Why it works**: Anxiety escalates through rumination. These steps interrupt the spiral.

### Post-Meeting Decompression (30-60 min, right after meeting ends)

**Steps**:
1. **Don't go straight back to work** — Don't sit at desk
2. **Walk outside** (minimum 15-30 min) — Movement helps process anxiety
3. **No phone/email** — Let your mind settle; don't check messages
4. **Light task on return** — Email sorting, file organization (mechanical work)
5. **No decisions** — Don't make any decisions for 30 min after returning
6. **Return to baseline before next task**

**Why it works**: Adrenaline from anxiety needs to discharge. Walking is proven to help.

## Part 6: What Meetings to Accept/Decline

### ACCEPT (Your 2-3 per week):

- **1-on-1s with your manager** (essential; can't decline)
- **Critical project meetings** (where your input is essential)
- **Client/stakeholder meetings** (if unavoidable; batch them)
- **Key decisions** (where your voice is required)

### DECLINE or REQUEST ASYNC ALTERNATIVE:

- **Standups** (you'll read async notes)
- **Optional all-hands** (you'll watch recording)
- **Recurring status meetings** (you'll provide written update)
- **"Quick syncs"** (usually not that quick; ask for async instead)
- **Meetings with unclear agendas** (ask to reschedule when clearer, with written agenda)
- **Back-to-back meetings** (no recovery between; negotiate spacing)

### BATCH (If you must have multiple):

Ideal: 1 meeting per day.
If unavoidable: 2 meetings with long break (lunch + recovery) between.

```
❌ BAD: 10am meeting, 11am meeting (no recovery)
✓ OKAY: 11am meeting, 2pm meeting (1-hour lunch between, partial recovery)
✓✓ GOOD: 11am meeting (recovery block), 3pm meeting next day (full day between)
```

## Part 7: If You Can't Limit Meetings (Worst Case)

If your role legitimately requires 4-5+ meetings per week, **you need different support**:

### Option 1: AI Augmentation
- AI attends some meetings and summarizes decisions
- You review async, give feedback
- Reduces your real-time meeting load

### Option 2: Delegation
- Delegate some meetings to a trusted colleague
- You review their notes and decisions
- Focus on only critical meetings

### Option 3: Role Redesign
- Restructure your role to reduce meeting-heavy tasks
- Move interrupt-driven work to someone else
- Protect your time for deep work

### Option 4: Honest Conversation
- Talk to your manager: "This role requires 5+ meetings per week. I have high meeting anxiety. This is unsustainable."
- Negotiate: Reduce meeting load OR provide support (AI, delegation) OR adjust expectations

**The hard truth**: If your role requires constant meetings and you have high meeting anxiety, something has to give. Pushing through with willpower doesn't work. You need systemic change.
