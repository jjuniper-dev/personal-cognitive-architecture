# AI Governance and Procurement Roles in Canada

## 1. Automated Decision-Making (ADM) Responsibilities

Under the Directive on Automated Decision-Making, duties are split between operational management and institutional oversight.

### Assistant Deputy Minister (ADM)

- **Algorithmic Impact Assessment (AIA)**: Completes and publishes the mandatory assessment before a system goes live.
- **Transparency**: Ensures citizens receive plain-language notices and explanations for automated decisions.
- **Quality Assurance**: Tests for bias and monitors system accuracy to prevent human rights violations.
- **Training**: Ensures all staff involved receive mandatory, recurring training on AI ethics and operations.
- **Recourse**: Establishes clear pathways for clients to challenge automated decisions.

### Deputy Minister (DM)

- **Final Authority**: Provides formal approval for high-impact systems (Levels III and IV) to enter production.
- **Institutional Compliance**: Responsible for the department's overall adherence to Treasury Board directives.
- **Legal Alignment**: Ensures AI systems comply with the Canadian Charter of Rights and Freedoms.
- **Strategic Planning**: Approves annual departmental plans for IT and data modernization.
